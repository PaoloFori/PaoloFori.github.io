Paolo Forin

Neurorobotics & Brain-Machine Interface Researcher
Motor Imagery · CVSA · ROS2 · Deep Learning

I build bridges between brains and machines. As a PhD candidate at UniPD, I design real-time BCI systems using endogenous paradigms — motor imagery and covert visuospatial attention — to let people control robotic arms, navigate virtual environments, and interact with the world in entirely new ways. A C++/Python engineer at heart, with a soft spot for elegant Linux setups and well-containerised code.

Real-time BCI based on mean power features and QDA classification for Covert VisuoSpatial Attention, with real audio feedback. Includes calibration and evaluation phases. Used as the basis for a published study.

Real-Time Collaborative BCI for Robotic Arm
Multi-user BCI framework where two operators collaboratively control a robotic arm in real time, merging decoded intentions through a shared control architecture.

BCI with an attentional gate based on a GMM model to detect whether the subject is focused or not. Paper accepted at Graz BCI Conference 2026, extended version submitted to IEEE SMC.
